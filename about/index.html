About

Currently I am in my final year of the master's program in the Department of Statistics and Actuarial Science at Simon Fraser University where I work with Luke Bornn. My research focuses on machine learning and spatio-temporal modeling with applications in basketball analytics. My first project was titled The Van Exel Effect: Adjusting for Scorekeeper Bias in NBA Box Scores and was featured as a poster presentation at the 2016 MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference.

Before coming to SFU, I obtained a BSc in Mathematics and Statistics at Acadia University. There I worked with Hugh Chipman, Pritam Ranjan, and Richard Karsten using Gaussian Process models to model potential energy generation surfaces of tidal energy turbines placed in the Minas Passage in the Bay of Fundy. This research resulted in my undergraduate thesis.
