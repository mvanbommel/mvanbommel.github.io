Basketball Analytics

My current research focuses on applying statistics and machine learning techniques to basketball analytics to gain insight into the game. The work has primarily been conducted in collaboration with Luke Bornn and the other students in the Bornn Lab, with a four month aside completing an internship in the Basketball Analytics & Strategy Department of the Philadelphia 76ers.

The Data

The main source of data for my research is optical player tracking data from the SportVu camera system. This data indicates the (x,y) location of all ten players on the court as well as the (x,y,z) location of the ball 25 times per second for all teams since the 2013-2014 NBA season. Quantifying NBA Scorekeeper Bias

Inspired by the NBA rescinding a few notable triple doubles and a Deadspin article revealing the statistic altering confessions of an NBA scorekeeper, I set out to quantify scorekeeper bias and specifically to determine if some NBA players are aided by biased scorekeeping, especially since the scorekeepers are hired by the home team. To measure this bias, I used a logistic regression model to predict the probability of every pass before a made basket to be recorded as an assist, including coefficients to measure player skill, spatio-temporal context, and scorekeeper influence. After finding results indicating bias among scorekeepers, I developed a method to adjust statistics to remove scorekeeper influence and also examined the potential impact of scorekeeper bias on the daily fantasy sports industry.
